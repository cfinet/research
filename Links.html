DISPATCHES...

  • Mitotic cell rounding accelerates epithelial invagination by Takefumi Kondo and Shigeo Hayashi (2013) Nature 494: 125-129.
    This paper shows that mitosis plays an active role in the epithelial invagination of the Drosophila melanogaster tracheal placode.


  • Souvenir from Roscoff (CJM "Emergence and evolution of developmental patterns", April 2012). Left row from back: Nicolas Denans, Priti Roy, Cédric Finet; right row from back: Qiyan Mao, David Caballero Vila, Ayan Ray, Flavien Caraguel.



  • Differential positioning of adherens junctions is associated with initiation of epithelial folding by Yu-Chiun Wang, Zia Khan, Matthias Kaschube, Eric Wieschaus (2012) Nature 484: 390-393.
    This paper describes how differential positioning of adherens junctions can play a role in the initiation of epithelial folding. This newly described mechanism appears as an alternative mechanism to myosin-dependent apical constriction.


  • A regulatory archipelago controls hox genes transcription in digits by Thomas Montavon, Natalia Soshnikova, Bénédicte Mascrez, Elisabeth Joye, Laurie Thevenet, Erik Splinter, Wouter de Laat, François Spitz, Denis Duboule (2011) Cell 147: 1132-1145.
    Beautiful example of how multiple regulatory elements can interact with each other to promote quantitatively and qualitatively the expression of developmental genes. One of my favorite 2011 papers in the field of developmental biology!!!


  • The same Regulatory Point Mutation Changed Seed-Dispersal Structures in Evolution and Domestication by Nicolas Arnaud, Tom Lawrenson, Lars stergaard, Robert Sablowski (2011) Current Biology 21: 1215-1219.
    This elegant paper shows how a single-nucleotide change in a conserved cis-regulatory element of REPLUMLESS could explain developmental variation in seed-dispersal structures.


  • Histoire Naturelle by Georges-Louis Leclerc de Buffon
    Probably the best way to understand the paradox of Buffon as formulated by Ernst Mayr: "He was not an evolutionist, yet he was the father of evolutionism."


  • Episodic radiations in the fly tree of life by Brian Wiegmann, Michelle Trautwein, Isaac Winkler, Norman Barr, Jung-Wook Kim, Christine Lambkin, Matthew Bertone, Brian Cassel, Keith Bayless, Alysha Heimberg, Benjamin Wheeler, Kevin Peterson, Thomas Pape, Bradley Sinclair, Jeffrey Skevington, Vladimir Blagoderov, Jason Caravas, Sujatha Kutty, Urs Schmidt-Ott, Gail Kampmeier, Christian Thompson, David Grimaldi, Andrew Beckenbach, Gregory Courtney, Markus Friedrich, Rudolf Meier, David Yeates (2011) PNAS 108: 5690-5695.
    This paper releases an updated version of the Diptera phylogeny. Such a beautiful and very useful work!


  • Morphological evolution and embryonic developmental diversity in metazoa by Isaac Salazar-Ciudad (2010) Development 137: 531-539.
    In this paper, the author proposes a classification of developmental types, as well as testable hypotheses regarding morphological evolution of body plans.


  • An actomyosin-based barrier inhibits cell mixing at compartmental boundaries in Drosophila embryos by Bruno Monier, Anne Pélissier-Monier, Andrea Brand, Bénédicte Sanson (2010) Nature Cell Biology 12: 60-65.
    This study shows that local regulation of actomyosin contractibility is the primary mechanism sorting cells at compartmental boundaries.
